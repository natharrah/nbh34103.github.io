Not so lucky year in Ireland

After I graduated Walter Hines Page High School in 2014 I felt compelled to take a gap year doing youth ministries. I traveled to a very small town called Dromore and lived on my own. Coming from a city of about 300,000 people was a big difference to coming to a town of only about 5,000. This was a huge change for me and honestly one of the biggest challenges I had there. Another big challenge I had to face was the lack of people my age in the town. This town was one that was very oppressive and once people turned 18 they typically moved out of the whole country and moved to England. Though those were my two biggest challenges, I thoroughly enjoyed the youth in the town. I made really great relationships with them that I know will last a life time. It was amazing how I brought a new light to religion to all the young people. Here is a link to my blog that I created during my gap year. www.natharrahyfc.weeby.com
